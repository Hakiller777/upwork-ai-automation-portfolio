# AcmeCRM — SLA & Support Tiers

## Support channels

- In-app chat widget (bottom-right corner of the web app)
- Email: support@acmecrm.example
- Phone support (Enterprise plans only)
- Status page: status.acmecrm.example, for incident updates

## Response time by plan

| Plan       | First response (chat/email) | Critical incident response |
|------------|------------------------------|------------------------------|
| Starter    | Within 24 business hours     | Best effort                  |
| Growth     | Within 8 business hours      | Within 4 hours               |
| Enterprise | Within 2 business hours      | Within 1 hour, 24/7          |

## Severity levels

- **Critical** — the product is down or data loss is occurring for the
  whole workspace.
- **High** — a core feature (e.g. pipeline board, email sync) is broken for
  most users.
- **Normal** — a non-blocking bug or a "how do I..." question.
- **Low** — a feature request or cosmetic issue.

## Uptime commitment

Enterprise plans include a 99.9% uptime SLA measured monthly, with service
credits for missed targets as described in the Enterprise Master Services
Agreement.

## Escalating a ticket

If a ticket hasn't received a response within its SLA window, reply to the
ticket email with "ESCALATE" in the subject line, or ask the in-app chat
bot to connect you with a human agent — either path immediately raises the
ticket's priority for the support team.

## Scheduled maintenance

Planned maintenance windows are announced at least 72 hours in advance on
the status page and by email to workspace Admins, and are scheduled outside
business hours in the account's configured timezone whenever possible.
