# AcmeCRM — Contact Management

## Contact records

A contact record stores name, email, phone, title, company, custom fields,
linked deals, and a full timeline of activities (emails, calls, meetings,
notes). Contacts can be linked to a Company record if you track
organizations separately from individual people.

## Creating contacts

Add a contact manually from Contacts → New Contact, import a batch via CSV
(see Data Import & Export), or let AcmeCRM auto-create one the first time
you email an unrecognized address through a connected mailbox.

## Merging duplicate contacts

Select two or more contacts from the list view and choose "Merge." AcmeCRM
suggests which record should be the primary and lets you review field-by-
field which value to keep before confirming — the merge cannot be undone,
so review carefully.

## Companies

A Company record groups multiple contacts that work at the same
organization, useful for B2B selling where you deal with several
stakeholders. Company-level fields (like industry or employee count) apply
to every linked contact.

## Segmenting contacts

Use list view filters (by custom field, source, owner, or activity
recency) to build segments, and save them as a view for quick access later.
Saved views can also be used as the audience for a scheduled report.

## Contact ownership and visibility

Every contact has an owner, and visibility follows the role rules described
in Admin Roles & Permissions — Reps typically only see contacts they own
unless a Manager grants broader visibility.

## Deleting a contact

Deleting a contact also removes it from any deals it was linked to as the
primary contact, but does not delete the deals themselves. Deleted contacts
go to a recovery trash for 30 days before permanent deletion.
